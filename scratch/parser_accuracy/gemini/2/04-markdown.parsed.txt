This table illustrates the critical trade-offs between speed, reliability, and observability in automation design. An aggressive controller prioritizes throughput by minimizing delays from
timeouts and retries, making it suitable for high-volume, non-critical tasks where some failures are tolerable. Conversely, a cautious controller ensures maximum reliability for mission-critical
operations through extensive logging, long timeouts, and robust retry mechanisms, at the cost of slower performance. The balanced approach offers a practical middle ground, providing good
reliability and diagnostic capabilities without sacrificing too much speed, making it a common choice for general-purpose automation.
