> In two sentences, explain why capturing both raw transcripts and cleaned
parser output is useful when verifying an automation pipeline.

● Raw transcripts preserve the complete, unmodified data stream including
  all control characters, escape sequences, and timing artifacts, which
  allows you to debug parsing failures and verify nothing important was lost
   during processing. Cleaned parser output shows what your automation
  actually extracted and will act upon, so comparing both reveals whether
  your parser correctly identifies command boundaries, response completion,
  and prompt states without introducing false positives or missing critical
  information.
